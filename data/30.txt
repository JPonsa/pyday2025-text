Indian Biryani: Layered Rice and Meat Perfection

Biryani represents the pinnacle of Indian culinary artistry, combining fragrant rice, spiced meat, and aromatic herbs into layered perfection. This Mughal-era dish varies regionally but follows fundamental techniques.

Essential Ingredients:
- Basmati rice (best quality, aged)
- Meat: chicken, goat, or beef
- Full-fat yogurt
- Saffron soaked in milk
- Fried onions (birista)
- Mixed nuts and raisins
- Fresh herbs: mint, cilantro

Spice Blend:
- 2 teaspoons cumin seeds
- 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
- 4 green cardamom pods
- 2 black cardamom pods
- 4 cloves
- 1 inch cinnamon stick
- 1 star anise
- 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg

Marinade (4 hours minimum):
- 1 kg meat pieces
- 1 cup yogurt
- 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
- 1 teaspoon turmeric
- 2 teaspoons red chili powder
- Fried onions
- Fresh mint and cilantro

Rice Preparation:
1. Soak 3 cups basmati rice 30 minutes
2. Boil 8 cups water with whole spices
3. Add rice, cook until 70% done
4. Drain immediately, spread to cool

Layering Process:
1. Marinated meat as bottom layer
2. Half-cooked rice layer
3. Saffron milk drizzle
4. Fried onions, nuts, herbs
5. Repeat layers
6. Seal pot tightly with dough or foil

Cooking Methods:
Traditional: Dum cooking over low heat for 45 minutes
Modern: Oven at 180Â°C for 30-40 minutes

Serving:
- Let rest 10 minutes before opening
- Fluff rice with fork
- Serve with raita and mirchi ka salan

Regional Variations:
Hyderabadi: Kacchi (raw meat) method
Lucknowi: Pakki (cooked meat) method
Kolkata: Potato and egg additions
Malabar: Short-grain rice, more spices
